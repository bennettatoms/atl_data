Metro Area Migration and Population

Atlanta is a vibrant and ever-evolving city whose population has been growing apace for decades, through downturns and up. Those who choose relocate to Atlanta are attracted by a number of factors, chief among which is the rare combination of thriving commerce and economic opportunity with below-national-average cost of living.


We've chosen to focus on the five counties that comprise the geographic, economic, and cultural heart of the city of Atlanta: Fulton, Dekalb, Gwinnett, Cobb, and Clayton. These five counties tally more than 3.6 million inhabitants, well over half of the vastly larger 20-county metro area.

Development

Atlanta is a city steeped in history, having emerged from Civil War ruin to sit at the heart of the Civil Rights movement a century later. And the story of the city can be told through her buildings.


In recent years, the Great Recession severely dampened development in Atlanta, but as you can see from the next two visualizations, momentum is building for another era of strong growth in the metro area.
